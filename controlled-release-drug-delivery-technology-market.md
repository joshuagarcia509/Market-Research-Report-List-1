<p><p>Controlled-release drug delivery technology regulates the release of therapeutic agents over time, enhancing efficacy and minimizing side effects. Key types include the Wurster technique, which employs fluidized bed coating for uniform particle coating; coacervation, which creates microcapsules through phase separation; and microencapsulation, encapsulating drugs in polymers. Implants provide long-term release directly into the bloodstream, while transdermal systems deliver drugs through the skin. Targeted delivery focuses on specific cells or tissues, increasing drug concentration at the intended site, complemented by other innovative methods.</p></p>
