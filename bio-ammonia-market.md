<p><ul><li>Ammonia from Corn Biomass</li><li>Ammonia from Woody Biomass</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Bio Ammonia market comprises two main types based on feedstock: ammonia from corn biomass and ammonia from woody biomass. Ammonia from corn biomass utilizes starch-rich crops as a primary raw material, leveraging fermentation and gasification processes to produce ammonia sustainably. In contrast, ammonia from woody biomass focuses on converting lignocellulosic materials, such as wood residues, into ammonia, often through thermochemical methods. Both pathways highlight the transition towards renewable sources for ammonia production, catering to environmental sustainability goals.</p></p>
