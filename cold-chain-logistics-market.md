<p><p>Cold chain logistics encompasses the transport of temperature-sensitive goods through various modes: airways, roadways, and seaways. Airways offer rapid delivery for perishable items, ensuring minimal temperature fluctuation during transit. Roadways provide flexible and accessible transport options, crucial for short to medium distances, often involving last-mile deliveries. Seaways, while slower, can handle bulk shipments and large quantities, making them cost-effective for long-distance transportation. Each mode facilitates the preservation of product integrity, supporting industries like food, pharmaceuticals, and biotechnology.</p></p>
